   MP supports Barnado's ‘Make a Difference Day’  

Richard Bacon MP at Barnado’s
Diss with from left, Manager
Kim Snelgrove and volunteer
Freda Howard
South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon and his wife Victoria took part in Barnado’s “Make a Difference Day”, an annual campaign organised by Community Service Volunteers (CVS) to encourage people to discover how worthwhile volunteering can be. 

Mr Bacon spent Saturday morning at Barnado’s in Mere Street, Diss serving at the till alongside Freda Howard, a volunteer with over 20 years experience in helping Barnado’s. Under the guidance of Shop Manager Kim Snelgrove, Mr Bacon also tried his hand with pressing clothes with a hand steamer. Mrs Bacon helped long-serving volunteer Pat King to sort and price children’s books.

Money raised from Barnado’s in Diss goes towards funding the charity’s work. Barnado’s works with around 110,000 children, young people and their families in over 383 specialised projects in local communities across the UK to overcome urgent issues of poverty, homelessness, disability, bereavement and abuse.

Mr Bacon said: “Victoria and I had a most enjoyable time at Barnado’s in Diss and we now have a better understanding of the hard work which goes into running a charity shop such as Barnado’s. I congratulate Kim Snelgrove and her team of volunteers for doing such an excellent job. I would urge anyone with a bit of time to spare to consider volunteering for a worthwhile charity such as Barnado’s.”
 

27 October 2007
