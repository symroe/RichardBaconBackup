  MP congratulates campaigners as Hempnall windfarm is rejected
  

 
The proposals for a windfarm at
Hempnall were unanimously rejected
 

Read Mr Bacon's letter to Hempnall Councillor Michael Windridge

 

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has congratulated local activists for their successful campaign against a windfarm in Hempnall. 

Mr Bacon said: “I am delighted at this decision and I congratulate all the local activists involved in this campaign”. 

“These plans would have presented  all kinds of problems for local residents and I don’t think that industrial-scale wind power belongs in a gentle rural landscape”. 

 
 


“The hard work put in by local campaigners has paid off and I am pleased that the district council recognised the overwhelming local opposition to these proposals”.

Mr Bacon was speaking after South Norfolk District Council unanimously rejected the application for a seven-turbine windfarm on Bussey's Loke in Hempnall. 

Mr Bacon wrote to Hempnall District Councillor Michael Windridge to give his support to campaigners ahead of the decision by South Norfolk District Council's planning committee

Mr Bacon wrote to the council's planning officers in May 2008 to register his objections to the scheme.

6 August 2008
