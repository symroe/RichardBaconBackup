MP applauds DCLG action on Upper Vaunces Farm


MINISTERS AT the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) have confirmed that the final decision on a three turbine windfarm on agricultural land near Rushall will be taken by the Communities Secretary Eric Pickles.

Following South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon’s strong representations to DCLG on behalf of local residents, Planning Minister Kris Hopkins confirmed in a letter to Mr Bacon that the appeal would be recovered by his department.  This means that the final decision on the windfarm will be taken by Communities Secretary Eric Pickles rather than a planning inspector. 

Although the planning inquiry on the proposed wind farm concluded in January, Section 79 of the Town and Country Planning Act 1990 empowers the Secretary of State to recover a planning appeal for his own determination at any point up until an inspector issues a decision. 

Mr Bacon said today: “I am very pleased that Mr Hopkins has recovered this appeal so that Eric Pickles can take the final decision. 

“It is bad for democracy if planning decisions taken locally are overridden by national policies at appeal.  The whole purpose of localism is to put give local communities greater control over local decisions like housing and planning.  Eric Pickles can look at these issues in the round and come to a balanced conclusion that gives proper weight to local views”.

TCI Renewables had already appealed to the Planning Inspectorate on the grounds that South Norfolk Council had taken too long to reach a decision on its application. 

14 April 2014
