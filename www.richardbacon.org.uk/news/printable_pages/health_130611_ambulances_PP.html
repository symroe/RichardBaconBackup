Report shows Ambulance Trust is a “Well-Funded Basket Case”, says MP

  Richard Bacon MP comments: 
The East of England Ambulance Trust has been allowed to degenerate into a well-funded basket case.  Rather than take swift and decisive action, the Board has been caught in a spiral of paralysis and decline.  There are many superb staff working for the Ambulance Trust and they deserve better leadership than this.   But the Trust’s own turnaround plan, announced to much fanfare earlier this year, has become three different plans which do not fit together.  		 

Commenting on the publication of the review into the East of England Ambulance Service Trust (EEAST) by Dr Anthony Marsh, Chief Executive of the West Midlands Ambulance Service Trust, South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon said:

“I welcome Dr Marsh’s thorough and candid report, which shows how the East of England Ambulance Trust has been allowed to degenerate into a well-funded basket case. 

“Rather than take swift and decisive action, the Board has been caught in a spiral of paralysis and decline.  There are many superb staff working for the Ambulance Trust and they deserve better leadership than this.   But the Trust’s own turnaround plan, announced to much fanfare earlier this year, has become three different plans which do not fit together.

“It is appalling that the Trust doesn’t appear to know for sure either how many frontline staff it actually employs or how many it will need . The Trust also needs to re-think the bizarre notion that what it needs right now is to recruit £350,000 worth of non-operational staff.  With around 400 front-line vacancies, there must be better ways to spend this money.

“I do not believe the Trust Board has the ability to drive through Dr Marsh’s recommendations in an robust, effective manner.  I have therefore written to the Health Secretary to urge him to deploy a transformational turnaround team to see the Trust through this difficult period”. 

11 June 2013
