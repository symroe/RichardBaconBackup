  MP challenges ministers on
Cygnet House
 

 
Richard Bacon MP challenges health
ministers to listen to public consultations
 over Norfolk dementia services
  

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has challenged health ministers in Parliament over the future of Cygnet House in Long Stratton.

Cygnet House is one of five Norfolk care facilities for the elderly with mental health problems which has been threatened with closure due to a review by the Norfolk and Waveney Mental Health Partnership NHS trust.

Speaking during health questions  in  the  House  of

 
 

Commons on Tuesday 22 March, Mr Bacon asked  health minister Stephen Ladyman about the public consultation process in relation to Cygnet House and the future of mental health services in Norfolk.

Mr Bacon said: “At its consultation meeting on 21 February, the Norfolk and Waveney mental health trust met with an overwhelming response from people in South Norfolk that Cygnet House must be kept open in some form, and not go the way of Dennyholme, an excellent facility in Diss which was tragically closed. Does the minister accept that if public consultation means anything, it must mean listening to what local people actually say?”.

In his answer the minister acknowledged that the mental health trust would have to take account of strong views held by the public locally. The consultation process ends on 15 April, after which there will 8 to 10 weeks before the trust reports back the results.

Speaking later Mr Bacon said: “Treating elderly people with mental health problems at home will not always work and in many cases is simply not an option. The mental health trust has talked of a 'vision' for improving mental health services in Norfolk. People will not be interested if this 'vision' involves closing excellent facilities like Cygnet House”.

“There was no evidence that the public were more concerned about treating young people with depression, for example, than safeguarding Cygnet House. The trust must find a way of using facilities such as Cygnet House more flexibly for a wider range of purposes. In particular, the trust must take account of the views which were actually expressed by the public, not the views they wish they had heard”.
