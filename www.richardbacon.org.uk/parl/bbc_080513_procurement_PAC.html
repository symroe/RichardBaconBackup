MP tells BBC “open your books”


outh Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has called on the BBC Trust to open up the corporation's accounts to the National Audit Office.

Mr Bacon said: “The BBC expects to receive public funds through the licence fee, yet it continues to duck calls for total transparency over how it is using public money”.

“No other body in receipt of public funds gets to pick and choose how its spending is scrutinised, and the BBC Trust have yet to present a single convincing reason why they should be any different”.

“The BBC should now open its books to the National Audit Office”.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the Commons public accounts committee published its report on BBC procurement. In January 2007, the BBC Trust replaced the Board of Governors as the body overseeing the BBC Executive. However, the report finds that despite the establishment of the Trust, BBC spending remains closed to proper independent scrutiny.

13 May 2008
