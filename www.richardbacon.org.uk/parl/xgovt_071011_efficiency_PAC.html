Government ‘sloppy’ on efficiency gains, says MP


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has described the government’s attitude to making efficiency gains as ‘sloppy’ after a new report finds that there is doubt over whether nearly three-quarters of the government’s reported £13.3 billion in efficiency gains are genuine or accurate.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the Commons public accounts committee published its report on the government’s efficiency programme.  The government’s efficiency programme aims to make efficiency gains of £21.5 billion a year by 2007-08. However, although government departments had reported £13.3 billion of annual efficiency gains up to 30 September 2006, the report finds that departments cannot show whether almost £10 billion of these gains are genuine. There is also uncertainty around whether gains have been measured without taking account of additional costs.

Mr Bacon said today: “This approach to reporting efficiency gains is just plain sloppy. If the government was serious about hitting its £21.5 billion target, it would not present a figure that does not bear close inspection. The government needs to look at this again and come up with more credible figures”.

11 October 2007
