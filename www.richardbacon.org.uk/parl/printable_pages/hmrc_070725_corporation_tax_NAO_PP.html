MP urges taxman to change his ways


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has called on HM Revenue and Customs to change its operational culture, as a new report finds that 58 per cent of its enquiries into large business Corporation Tax were expected to yield less than one per cent in additional tax yield, but that its tax specialists are reluctant to stop such enquiries.

Mr Bacon said today: “HM Revenue and Customs is expending too much energy pursuing small amounts of Corporation Tax with little chance of a decent return.  The Department must change its operational culture so that it works more closely with large businesses and shifts its focus to high levels of tax at risk.”

Mr Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, was speaking as the National Audit Office (NAO) published its report into the management of large business Corporation Tax. 

The report finds that in February 2007 HMRC was carrying out 4,700 enquiries, 58 per cent of which were expected to produce less than one per cent of the total additional tax yield generated from compliance activities. 

The culture of opening enquiries even where low amounts of tax are at risk has resulted in large numbers of open enquiries and that tax specialists have shown reluctance to stop these enquiries.

25 July 2007
