'Don't bounce dentists,' warns MP

SOUTH NORFOLK MP Richard Bacon has warned health chiefs not to 'bounce' dentists into accepting a revolutionary new payment structure for NHS dental work without proper consultation, following the publication of a report by the influential Commons public accounts committee on reforming NHS dentistry.

Mr Bacon, a member of the committee, said: "The changes to the charging system are radical and ministers have left very little time to win over the dental profession before the new scheme comes into operation in April 2006. Any attempt to 'bounce' already sceptical dentists into the new structure could spark an exodus from the NHS, killing the Government's reforms stone dead and possibly even NHS dentistry itself".

The report also finds that the move away from the current charging system of 400 different items of treatment to a smaller system of 3 price bands could risk creating incentives for dentists to offer private work to NHS patients at a lower cost, potentially leading to a fall in the amount of money recovered by the NHS.

Mr Bacon added: "The clock is ticking on these reforms and the Government has set itself a punishing timetable that owes far more to hope than to experience. Ministers must make sure that dentists, patients and local health trusts, are content with the new arrangements. If they do not, NHS dentistry faces becoming a contradiction in terms".

14 July 2005
