‘Meek’ HMRC must get tougher with global firms, says MP

Commenting on the Commons public accounts committee’s report into the 2011-12 accounts of HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC), South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon, a member of the committee, said:

“For many years, senior tax officials have meekly accepted the way in which major multinationals use complicated cross-border transactions to avoid paying their fair share of tax.  Big companies can afford the advice of expensive lawyers and accountants to take advantage of marketed avoidance schemes whose basis is often entirely artificial.”

“The velvet glove with which HM Revenue and Customs treats these firms sits in stark contrast with the iron fist experienced daily by small businesses and individuals.  Ordinary taxpayers are rightly infuriated by HMRC’s perceived lack of even-handedness and HMRC should get tougher with big global firms.”

“Any tax system requires the broad confidence of taxpayers and HMRC will only regain this confidence if it is seen by the wider taxpaying public to be acting without fear or favour.   HMRC must work closely with the Treasury and do much more to challenge, censure and prevent the abuse of techniques such as transfer pricing and royalty payments which enable multinationals to reduce their UK tax exposure to levels that are unfair to everyone else”.   

3 December 2012
