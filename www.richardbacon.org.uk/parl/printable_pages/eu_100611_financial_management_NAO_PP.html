Still too much error in EU accounts, says MP

COMMENTING ON the National Audit Office report on financial management in the European Union, Richard Bacon, MP for South Norfolk, said:

“The National Audit Office states that there has been a ‘detectable improvement’ in the EU’s financial management, which is not before time. However, for the fifteenth year in a row, the EU has failed to provide accounts for which the European Court of Auditors can provide a positive Statement of Assurance on whether the underlying transactions conform to applicable laws and regulations.

“There continues to be an unacceptably high level of error. This is because EU schemes are just too complicated. The government should press at every opportunity for EU schemes to made simpler to operate, with clear measurable objectives.

“Poorly designed EU schemes such as the Single Farm Payment Scheme continue to hurt British taxpayers as well as being a nightmare for farmers. The EU needs to work much harder to safeguard the money that taxpayers across Europe entrust to it”. 

11 June 2010
