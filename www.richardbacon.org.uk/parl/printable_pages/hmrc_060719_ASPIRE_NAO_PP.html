Omens for tax computers not good, says MP

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has expressed concern over early signs that the cost of a new I.T. contract signed by HM Revenue and Customs may overrun.

Mr Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, was speaking as the National Audit Office (NAO) published its report into ASPIRE, a £3 billion 10-year contract with I.T. firm Capgemini to provide I.T. services for HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC).

HMRC signed the deal in January 2004, but its demand for I.T. services pushed ASPIRE’s first-year costs to £539.9 million - £155.5 million over estimates. 

The report finds that, if I.T. spending continues at this rate over the lifetime of the contract, the final cost for ASPIRE could be in excess of £6 billion. 

Mr Bacon, a member of the committee, said today:

“The omens for ASPIRE are not good, and costs could double if spending on I.T. services continues at the present rate.  HMRC’s track record with computer systems is poor, to say the least. Officials need to make sure their plans to reduce demand for I.T. services actually happen, otherwise we will be left with yet another I.T. contract with wildly escalating costs”.

5 April 2011
