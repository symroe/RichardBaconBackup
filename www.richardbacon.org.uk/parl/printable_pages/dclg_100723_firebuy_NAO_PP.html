Fire equipment body must improve rapidly, says MP

COMMENTING ON the publication of the National Audit Office report into Firebuy Ltd., a specialist body set up by the Department for Communities and Local Government to help Fire and Rescue Services buy vehicles and equipment, Richard Bacon MP, a member of the Commons public accounts committee said:

“Firebuy, a body established by the Department for Communities and Local Government to help fire brigades buy equipment, has cost the taxpayer nearly twice as much to set up and run as the savings it claims to have delivered.  Firebuy was supposed to have been self financing by 2009, yet it is still heavily reliant on grants from the Department.  

“Firebuy needs to persuade England’s 46 fire brigades of the benefits of its services, but only five of Firebuy’s 14 procurement contracts have proved popular with local Fire and Rescue Services.  Furthermore, Firebuy has no information on when equipment contracts are due to expire, which fire brigades are choosing to buy elsewhere or even what products Firebuy has itself sold through its procurement contracts. 

“There are benefits in centralised procurement, but Firebuy is neither cheap enough nor successful enough to justify its continued existence.  Firebuy has failed to win over England’s Fire and Rescue Services and unless it improves rapidly the Department should pull the plug”. 

23 July 2010
