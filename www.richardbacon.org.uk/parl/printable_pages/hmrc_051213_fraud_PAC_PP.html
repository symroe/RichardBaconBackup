Tax fraud would pay for 100 years' policing, says MP

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has calculated that the amount of money the UK lost to fraud on VAT and on fuel duty in 2003-04 is enough to pay Norfolk’s policing bill for the next one hundred years.

Mr Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, was speaking as the committee’s report into the performance of HM Customs and Excise in 2003-04 was published.

The report finds that losses through VAT fraud, together with fraud on hydrocarbon oils excise duty, mainly petrol and diesel, cost the public £13 billion for the year in question – over 100 times the budget for Norfolk police of £127 million for 2005-06.  Alternatively, the losses could meet Norfolk’s education budget of £446 million for almost 30 years.

Mr Bacon said: “When you look at what these losses could actually pay for, you get some idea of the staggering amounts of money that are simply disappearing every year.  To make matters worse, Customs and Excise are still not dealing with this problem effectively”. 

“The quality of intelligence on fraud is patchy and few fraudsters have been brought to justice.  Even those few who are caught have received unacceptably low sentences, averaging just 15 months in prison.  HM Revenue and Customs needs to do a much better job of finding and punishing fraudsters if these enormous losses are to be stopped”.   

13 December 2005
