MP slams ‘absurd’ performance of Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority

SOUTH NORFOLK MP Richard Bacon has said that the management of the Criminal  Injuries Compensation Authority does not appear to be up to the job, as a new report finds the authority's performance has declined over the last seven years.

Mr Bacon said: “It is absurd but true that the average time to resolve a case has risen from 364 days to 515 days at a time when applications are down by 23 per cent.

“These figures tell a story of indifference bordering on sloth. The Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority is failing the victims of crime.

“The Authority is also failing taxpayers, who keep paying more even though it keeps achieving less. Victims of crime have to cope with more than enough trauma without also facing incompetence and red tape from those who are supposed to be helping them.

“Ministers need to sort out the management of the authority, which does not appear to be up to the job”.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the National Audit Office published its report into the Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (CICA), the non-departmental public body which pays statutory financial compensation to victims of violent crime.  CICA received 61,000 applications for compensation in 2006-07 and paid out £192 million to victims.

However, the report finds CICA’s performance has declined since the National Audit Office last reported on it in 2000.  The average time to resolve a case has increased by over 40 per cent, from 364 days in 1998-99 to 515 days in 2006-07, even though the number of applications CICA receives each year fell by 23 per cent over the same period.  Additionally, the number of cases CICA completed in a year fell from 74,900 in 1998-99 to 59,100 in 2006-07.  Over the same period, CICA’s administration costs increased by £4.2 million.
