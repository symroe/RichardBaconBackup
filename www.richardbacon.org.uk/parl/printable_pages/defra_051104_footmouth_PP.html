'Be Biosecure', MP urges farmers

SOUTH NORFOLK MP Richard Bacon has called for farmers to be vigilant in maintaining 'biosecure' farms if they want to avoid footing the bill for a future Foot and Mouth epidemic.

Mr Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, was speaking as the committee's report on the lessons learnt from the 2001 Foot and Mouth outbreak was published.  The report suggests that any future changes in the way the costs of an outbreak are met could be linked to standards in biosecurity maintained on a farm.

Mr Bacon said: “Ensuring good biosecurity is vital in preventing another outbreak of Foot and Mouth.  Most farmers are highly vigilant against disease, but anyone who tries to cut corners could put his own and others' livelihoods at risk.

“The Government must also do its share.  Last time DEFRA was woefully ill-prepared to deal with the foot and mouth outbreak. Although there may have been some improvement, there remain important areas for concern, such as the level of control over imported meat which is not always produced to our animal welfare standards and may be more prone to disease”.

The report also calls on the Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs to be sure that any national contingency plan to deal with another outbreak is matched with effective strategies by local authorities and emergency services.

Mr Bacon added: “I am pleased that DEFRA now has its national contingency plan in place, but the Government must ensure that relevant bodies at all levels have suitable plans in place too.  Given the enormous costs of the 2001 outbreak, we cannot afford to leave anything to chance”.

4 November 2005
