IT ‘Big Bang’ left CSA broken, says MP

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has called on officials to learn from a scathing report by the National Audit Office on the failure of the programme to reform the Child Support Agency (CSA).

Mr Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, was speaking as the National Audit Office (NAO) published its report into the way the CSA’s 2003 reform programme was implemented.

Three years after the £0.5 billion CSA reform programme, was introduced, the Agency’s performance has still not improved.  The programme was intended to start in March 2002, but was delayed by a year because the new IT system, CS2, had not been fully tested.  By the time the system was introduced a year later, CS2 had developed 14 critical defects. 

There are still some 500 faults with CS2 and 36,000 new cases are currently ‘stuck’ in the system because of IT failures.  Over 60 per cent of the CSA’s caseload has not benefited from the reforms and the CSA has recognised it did not have the resources to check effectively the system that EDS was delivering. 

Mr Bacon said today: “The Child Support reforms have cost a great deal more than expected and achieved a great deal less than hoped.  The Child Support Agency was already underperforming and overstretched when officials took the nuclear option and decided to bring in new IT systems whilst simultaneously making huge structural changes to the way the agency worked.  This was a recipe for disaster”. 

“When the dust settled from this ‘big bang’, it was discovered that the reforms had made matters far worse.  For example, it now costs the CSA 70p to collect every £1 of maintenance.  Small wonder government support for single parents and their children has become little short of a bad joke”. 

“It is clear that officials fell for EDS’s sales patter and had no means to check whether EDS were delivering what was promised.  Officials must learn from this report and take a more cautious approach to IT in future, but the damage is already done and many thousands of families have been left facing serious hardship as a result”. 

30 June 2006
