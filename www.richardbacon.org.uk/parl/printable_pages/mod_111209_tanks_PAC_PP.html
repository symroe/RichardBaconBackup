'Indecisive and over-ambitious' MoD has nothing to show for £1 billion armoured vehicle spending, says MP

“The MoD has been indecisive in setting vehicle requirements and over-ambitious in ordering expensive kit with all the bells and whistles”. Richard Bacon MP

Commenting on the Commons public accounts committee’s report into the Ministry of Defence’s procurement of armoured vehicles, South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon, a member of the committee, said:

“This report is yet another example of the MoD’s inability to live within its means.” 

“The MoD has nothing to show for the £1.1 billion of taxpayers’ money it has spent on armoured vehicle projects since 1998.   Around a third of this money was wasted on projects that have been cancelled or suspended. The MoD has been indecisive in setting vehicle requirements and over-ambitious in ordering expensive kit with all the bells and whistles. 

“Armoured vehicles have suffered more severe budget cuts than other equipment projects, mainly because they involve lower levels of contractual commitments and are therefore easier to cut.  As a result, there is not enough money left for the purchases of new tanks, reconnaissance vehicles and armoured personnel carriers which are planned.  This mismanagement forced the MoD to go cap in hand to the Treasury so it could buy mine-resistant vehicles for Iraq and Afghanistan at a cost of £2.8 billion. 

9 December 2011
