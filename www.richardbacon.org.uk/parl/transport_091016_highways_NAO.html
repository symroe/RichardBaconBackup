Highways Agency losing engineers while gaining admin staff, says MP


COMMENTING ON the publication of the National Audit Office’s report on contracting for highways maintenance, South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, said:

“It is worrying that the part of the Highways Agency responsible for maintenance has lost over a quarter of its engineers since 2004 yet has managed to find room for nearly 200 new admin staff over the same period.

“The Agency also uses very few quantity surveyors, but it is precisely the deep skills of staff such as engineers and quantity surveyors that are vital for effective contract management.

“The Agency must do more to make sure that the resources it has available are used in the most effective way for taxpayers”.

16 October 2009
