Treasury ‘not listening’ to PFI advice, says MP


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has said that the Treasury is not listening to good advice on how to improve the way PFI works, as a new report finds that little has changed since the last time Parliament examined the operation of the Private Finance Initiative.

Mr Bacon said: “There is plenty of good advice available on how to improve the way PFI works, but the Treasury doesn’t appear to be listening to it. As a result, PFI is proving an increasingly bad deal for taxpayers and fewer private sector firms have enough patience to see the process through”.

“If the Treasury continues to give the public sector few choices outside PFI for the funding of capital projects, it must put the lessons it has learned into effect.   Otherwise, taxpayers will get a raw deal from PFI”.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the Commons public accounts committee published its report into the PFI tendering process.  There are currently 800 Private Finance Initiative (PFI) contracts with private sector suppliers for services worth in total £155 billion up to 2032. 

However, four years after the Committee highlighted a number of issues regarding the PFI tendering process, the report finds there has been no improvement in tendering times and that there is a continuing lack of skills and experience within public sector PFI teams.  The report also finds that value for money is being put at risk when public authorities make late changes to PFI deals and, as a result, there are signs that market interest in PFI is weakening, with fewer serious bids for recent deals. 

27 November 2007
