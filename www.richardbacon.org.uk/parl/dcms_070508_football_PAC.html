MP wants football clubs to pay up for safety


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has called on big football clubs to pay the costs of running the Football Licensing Authority after a new report finds that each year taxpayers are paying £30,000 per person to accommodate the authority’s six staff at a top London address.

Mr Bacon, a member of the Commons public accounts committee, said: “Big football clubs have plenty of money. It is not obvious why taxpayers should have to pay £30,000 per person to keep the staff of the Football Licensing Authority at a top London address.

“Taxpayers should not be paying through the nose for posh office space for the Football Licensing Authority, whilst multi-million pound football clubs are rolling in money and being charged a pittance for vital safety certificates”.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the Commons public accounts committee published its report on the office accommodation of the Department for Culture, Media and Sport (DCMS).  The report finds that one body sponsored by DCMS, the Football Licensing Authority (FLA), which certifies the safety of football grounds, is paying £180,000 per year or £30,000 per person to accommodate its six staff in generous office space at Cavendish Square in London’s West End. 

The FLA charges England’s 94 clubs and stadia just £100 for a safety certificate, irrespective of a club’s size or status.  The fees are then all passed on to DCMS and the FLA receives no income from licence fees.

8 May 2007
