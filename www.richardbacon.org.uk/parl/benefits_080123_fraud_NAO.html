MP asks ministers to chase fraud losses


SOUTH NORFOLK MP Richard Bacon has asked ministers at the Department for Work and Pensions to explain why the DWP is only seeking to recover 42 per cent of outstanding fraud debt. 

Mr Bacon said: “It is not clear why the Department for Work and Pensions is seeking to recover less than half of the £800 million it lost to fraud in 2006-07.  The DWP is chasing £339 million in outstanding fraud debt, but the remaining £460 million seems to have gone for good.  The DWP needs to be much more ambitious in its counter-fraud efforts”. 

Mr Bacon was speaking as the National Audit Office published its report on progress in tackling benefit fraud.  The Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) estimates that it lost £800 million to fraud in 2006-07. 

However, the report finds that the DWP is only seeking to recover £339 million in known fraud debt, 42 per cent of the Department’s total losses to fraud.  This leaves £461 million in losses to fraudulent activity that the DWP is not pursuing. 

23 January 2008
