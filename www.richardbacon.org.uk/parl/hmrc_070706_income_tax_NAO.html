MP casts doubt on taxman's bank account plan


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has cast doubt on HM Revenue & Customs’ plan to take unpaid tax direct from peoples’ bank accounts as a new report to Parliament finds that the tax records of over one million people still contain errors.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the National Audit Office (NAO) published its report into processing income tax.

The report finds that errors in the tax system during 2006-07 caused an estimated £125 million of tax underpayments and £157 million in overpayments affecting around 1 million taxpayers.

Earlier this week, HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) proposed to take unpaid tax direct from the bank accounts of chronic defaulters, bypassing the need for a court order to seize the money.

Mr Bacon said today: “Given that over a million tax records contain errors, it is far from certain that HM Revenue and Customs should be allowed to interfere with the bank accounts of potentially innocent taxpayers. HMRC needs to iron out these errors first and should think twice before delving into peoples’ private bank accounts”.

6 July 2007
