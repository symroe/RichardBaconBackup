MoD's cost-cutting ‘being felt on the frontline’, says MP

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has said that the Ministry of Defence’s efforts to cut the cost of procurement  is having an impact on the frontline, as a new report finds that the MoD has chose to tackle cost increases by ordering less kit, reducing its capability or delaying its delivery.

Mr Bacon said: ‘The MoD’s attempts to keep a lid on cost increases are being felt on the front line as vital equipment is either cut back or delayed.”.

“Our troops are under huge pressures and yet are continuing to pay the price for the MoD’s inefficiencies. This is unacceptable”.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the National Audit Office published its report into the Ministry of Defence’s 20 largest military equipment projects.  The report finds that in order to limit cost increases the MoD has delayed delivery dates, reduced equipment capability or cut the quantities of equipment ordered.

The MoD has cut the number of anti-aircraft missiles being ordered for the Type 45 Destroyer, which is intended to form the backbone of the Royal Navy’s air defences.  The MoD has also cut back on communications equipment for the A400M transport plane and delayed the building of a training facility for A400M pilots. 

30 November 2007
