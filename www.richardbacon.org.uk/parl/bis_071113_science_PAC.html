Science costs face big bang, says MP


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has called on the new Department for Innovation, Universities and Skills to get a better grip on the costs of the UK’s big science facilities, as a new report finds that some operating cost estimates have increased by over 80 per cent.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the Commons public accounts committee published its report into large scientific facilities in the UK. The former Department of Trade and Industry and the UK’s Research Councils have allocated over £860 million to constructing 10 new large scientific facilities, including a new Antarctic Research Station and the Royal Research Ship James Cook.

However, the report finds that project teams have significantly underestimated the likely costs of operating the new facilities once they are up and running. For example the £383 million Diamond Synchotron, which is designed to study structures at a molecular and atomic level, has seen estimates of its operating costs balloon from £24.4 million to £46.1 million – an increase of 89 per cent.  

Mr Bacon said: “Big science facilities are of vital importance if the UK is to maintain its place at the cutting edge of science and discovery. However, there has been a ‘big bang’ in the rise in operating costs with some increasing by over 80 per cent. The new Department for Innovation, Universities and Skills must get a grip of these facilities’ operating costs”.

13 November 2007
