  MP urges minister to meet families of road traffic victims  

SOUTH NORFOLK MP Richard Bacon has called on roads minister Dr Stephen Ladyman to meet with the families of road traffic victims, following a debate on road traffic deaths on the A47 in the House of Commons this morning.

Mr Bacon said: “I was delighted to hear in this morning’s debate that Dr Ladyman intends to visit Norfolk and see our road traffic problems for himself. I have written to the minister thanking him for making this commitment and asking him if he will meet with the families of road traffic victims”.

 

“Along with my fellow Norfolk MPs, I am sure the minister would welcome the opportunity to meet with these families, as they are only too aware of the dangers of Norfolk’s most notorious roads”.

2 November 2005
