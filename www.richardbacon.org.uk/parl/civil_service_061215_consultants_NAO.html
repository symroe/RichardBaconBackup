Government consultants earning 'easy money' says MP


SOUTH NORFOLK MP Richard Bacon has described consultants who are working with central government departments as earning ‘easy money’, as a new report finds that government departments are regularly failing to monitor the performance of the consultants they hire.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the National Audit Office (NAO) published its report into central government’s use of consultants.  £7.2 billion has been spent on consultants over the last three years, but the NAO cannot say whether the government received value for money from its use of consultants because departments rarely collect information on what consultants have achieved. 

The report also finds that departments do not keep information on their use of consultancies and do not look to see whether they can use their own people before hiring consultants. 

Mr Bacon said today: “Consultants working with central government departments probably can’t believe their luck.  They are contracted to a job where hardly anybody collects information on what has been achieved and where performance is not routinely monitored”. 

“Consultants can certainly bring specialist skills and wide experience to public sector projects, but government departments are letting them make easy money.  The government must make sure it has a far better understanding of exactly what it is using consultants for, and whether or not such services could be provided in-house at a lower cost”. 

15 December 2006
