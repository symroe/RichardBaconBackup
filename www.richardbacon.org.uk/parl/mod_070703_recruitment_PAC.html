Troop shortage at crisis levels, says MP


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon has said the shortage of soldiers, sailors and airmen is now larger than the number of armed forces personnel currently deployed in Afghanistan.

Mr Bacon was speaking as the Commons public accounts committee published its report into recruitment and retention in the Armed Forces.  The report finds that the impact of continuous downsizing and overstretch is affecting the MoD’s ability to provide a satisfactory life for Armed Forces personnel and that this has lead to an increase in those leaving the Armed Forces early

Mr Bacon said: “It is worrying that the MoD currently needs to recruit more soldiers, sailors and airmen than it now has deployed in Afghanistan.  The level of damage to family life caused by increasing overseas deployment has reached crisis point for many servicemen and women.  Our troops do a superb job and it is a sign of failure that the MoD cannot hang on to them”.

“The MoD must provide stability and certainty for service personnel and their families. Otherwise, an increasing amount of experience, manpower and money will be lost”.

The report finds that, at April 2007, the Armed Forces’ overall recruitment shortfall was 5,850 personnel, meaning the MoD currently needs to recruit more people than are currently deployed in Afghanistan.  Past cuts in recruitment have had a damaging longer-term effect on manning in some areas, but budget constraints are preventing the MoD from recruiting enough to make up for the shortfall. 

3 July 2007
