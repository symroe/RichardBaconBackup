BACON BITS: Richard's monthly Diss Express column

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community.

SAFFRON APPRENTICESHIPS
One of the biggest problems facing any small business which is considering taking on apprentices is the extra administrative burden. Saffron Housing Trust may have found the answer. Members of the Diss Business Forum gathered recently to learn more about Saffron’s excellent apprenticeship scheme.

Saffron plans to give 20 local businesses the ability to take on their first apprentice next year and, by handling the administration, Saffron will free local businesses from much of the day-to-day worry involved in apprenticeships.  The apprentices will work four days a week and spend the fifth day studying. 

This is a simple, common sense idea with real potential and I will shortly be meeting the Apprenticeships Minister and West Suffolk MP Matt Hancock to draw his attention to the scheme.

HARLESTON NATIONWIDE
You may remember that I wrote in last month’s column about Nationwide’s intention to close its Harleston branch. 

After that I was joined by local business Harleston leaders to discuss these proposals on an hour-long conference call with senior Nationwide managers.   I will say that Nationwide listened carefully and considerately to our arguments, but unfortunately it became clear that the building society has no intention of changing its mind, which is a great pity. 

It is not easy for any building society or bank to make small branches work but the decision to close the Harleston branch still seems short-sighted, particularly as the town grows over the coming years. 

TAKING THE BULL BY THE HORNS
Earlier in the year, I wrote to NHS Norfolk and Waveney about care home fees but the response I got back was packed with jargon and almost unintelligible.  What on earth is a ‘negative financial uplift’?

It prompted me to nominate NHS Norfolk for the Plain English Campaign’s ‘Golden Bull’ award for worst written nonsense.  The Plain English Campaign recently declared NHS Norfolk and Waveney a Winner of a 2012 Golden Bull Award!

The awards are a bit of fun with a serious side.  When MPs write to organisations on behalf of our constituents, we want to be able to share the replies we receive, explain what they mean and give advice about what to do next.  That is much more difficult unless public bodies which serve the public use clear plain English.

CHRISTMAS WISHES
Finally, I’d like to take this opportunity to wish you all a very merry Christmas and a happy and prosperous 2013.

27 December 2012
