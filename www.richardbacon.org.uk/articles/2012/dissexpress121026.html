BACON BITS: Richard's monthly Diss Express column

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community.

THE VIKINGS ARE COMING!
I am sure that, like me, you are looking forward to the parade of the Royal Anglian Regiment in just under a fortnight.  

I am delighted that the Vikings are coming to Diss to be honoured with the Freedom of the Town and I want to pay tribute to Diss Town Council and Mayor Graham Minshull for their tireless work in making this parade happen.  Thanks must also go to Diss’s District Councillors, Keith Kiddie, Glyn Walden and Tony Palmer for their support.  

This is our chance to honour the brave men and women who have given so much in service of our country.  Their bravery and sacrifice is truly humbling and we are all tremendously proud of them. 

It will be a truly exceptional day and I hope to see you there.

ON THE BEAT
You may have seen me out and about on the streets of Diss recently in the company of Inspector Alice Scott, who is in charge of policing in the town.  

Inspector Scott, who began her policing career in Diss, told me first-hand about the issues facing the police locally, also introducing me to local businesses and organisations who are playing their part in keeping crime in Diss under control. 

I took this message into a recent meeting with Norfolk’s Chief Constable, who is a keen supporter of the community-based approach adopted by police officers in Diss.  Being a victim of crime can be a terrifying experience, but our police force generally does an excellent job and that’s why Norfolk is one of the safest places to live in the country. 

POLICE & CRIME COMMISSIONERS
Talking of the police, Norfolk’s voters will go to the polls on 15 November to choose the county’s first ever elected Police and Crime Commissioner.

The person who is elected will be responsible for deciding on Norfolk Police’s priorities and for setting the budget, including the police precept on your council tax bill.   However, the new Commissioner won’t be able to tell police officers who gets investigated or arrested.  Norfolk’s Chief Constable will stay in operational control of the force.

Elected police commissioners give the public a direct say in local policing and they will answer to you through the ballot box.   Don’t leave this decision to anyone else because they may be leaving it up to you.   Please make sure you vote on 15 November.

 

 

28 September 2012
