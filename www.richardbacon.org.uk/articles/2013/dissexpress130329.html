BACON BITS: Richard's monthly Diss Express column

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community.


AMBULANCE VISIT
Our ambulance service has been in turmoil recently, so I visited the Norwich ambulance control room to see operations for myself.  Given the local concern over Diss’s ambulance cover, I invited Mayor Graham Minshull to join me. 

We had a useful introduction to the way the service operates from control room managers.  Then I sat with a dispatcher to watch him deploy ambulances across our region.  I have no doubt about the commitment of frontline staff.  They do a terrific job under enormous pressure and we are all in their debt. However, I think they deserve much better leadership from the Trust Board.  It is also clear that targets are distorting management decisions, leading to greater frustration for staff.

It’s time to question whether one ambulance service can serve both outer London and rural East Anglia properly.  I don’t believe that it can. 

MATERNITY SERVICES
I want to thank everyone who contacted me with their experiences of NHS maternity services in Norfolk. 

It is clear from the response that new mums generally have high praise for the doctors, nurses and midwives who looked after them.  However, there’s also a big question over whether there’s enough support for mums-to-be in rural areas.  The need to travel long distances to get to Norwich can be both exhausting and expensive for expectant parents.  There’s also the question of capacity at the Norfolk & Norwich, which can often see women diverted to other hospitals in Norfolk or Suffolk at the 11th hour.

The contributions I received will be passed to the National Audit Office to inform their forthcoming report to Parliament.  Thank you again for your help. 

SELF BUILD
Like most things, housing operates on the principle of supply and demand.  Because the demand is high but the supply is low, houses quickly become very expensive to rent or buy. 

What we really need is more houses.  The are many beautiful villages in my constituency that would still be just as beautiful with a handful of extra houses, particularly for local people who grew up there.  In South Norfolk, just 0.6 percent of the land is housing, whereas 92 percent is countryside. 

I think part of the answer is to help people build a house themselves.  That’s why I’m launching the All Party Parliamentary Group on Self-Build.  My aim is to help make it much easier for people to get some land, get planning permission and build their own home. 

 

29 March 2013
