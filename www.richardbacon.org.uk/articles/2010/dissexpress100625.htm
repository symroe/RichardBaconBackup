Job Centre’s a good ’un

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community


I visited the Diss Jobcentre recently to hear about how the local employment market is faring.

Manager Ian Ward and Customer Services Operations Manager Caroline Green told me how the employment situation locally is now getting steadily better, with 92 per cent of people coming into Diss Jobcentre working again within a year. 

What surprised Jobcentre staff during the recession was the number of white collar workers coming through their door.  The biggest challenge that many jobseekers face is that they have forgotten how to ‘market’ themselves through their CV and so find job hunting a discouraging experience.

Being unemployed eats away at people’s self confidence and makes them forget their strengths.  That is why it is so important that Jobcentre Plus staff are supporting Jobseekers every step of the way and helping people to see that there is light at the end of the tunnel.    

Regional Development Agencies
At the time of writing, it looks very much as if regional development agencies are to be scrapped by the coalition government.  I know some people have found these quangos somewhat remote and unresponsive to Norfolk’s needs. 

Local Enterprise Partnerships are the means by which the government aims to put power back in our hands.  A Local Enterprise Partnership for Norfolk must bring together local businesses together with local councils to address the need for economic growth, new jobs and the regeneration of the deprived parts of Norfolk.  

This is potentially a huge opportunity which we need to grasp with both hands. 

RAF Marham

Norfolk has been a longstanding home to military airfields and last week, I visited the RAF Marham at the invitation of the station commander, Group Captain ‘Rocky’ Rochelle.

The airbase is home to several squadrons of Tornado fighter-bombers, including II (AC) Squadron, the oldest flying squadron in the world.

Richard Bacon MP (bottom) with
Squadron Leader 'Gaz' Littlechild

But the jets and their aircrew are just one part of the team. Each squadron is backed up by over 140 engineers, or ‘Gingers’ from the cockney rhyming slang Ginger Beers, and hundreds more men and women in almost every trade you can think of.

Group Captain Rochelle and his team do an amazing job and we should all be very proud of them. Many of the RAF Marham aircrew are shortly to deploy to Afghanistan, where they will be supporting NATO forces in battle against the Taliban. I wish them all Godspeed.
