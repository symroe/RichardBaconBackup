BACON BITS: Richard's monthly Diss Express column

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community.

RAIL IMPROVEMENTS
Our local rail operator, Greater Anglia, recently asked me what improvements in their services should be high on the list between now and 2016.  Greater Anglia already has new rolling stock as a top priority so this was an opportunity to bang the drum for other improvements. 

My reply urged Greater Anglia to protect and improve services from Diss station.  I also called for the journey between Norwich and London to be made quicker without cutting the number of station stops in between.  The seat reservation service also needs improvement so that reserved seats are marked.   

I’m pleased that Greater Anglia is looking for feedback from its passengers.  If any Diss Express readers have any suggestions which I can pass on to Greater Anglia, please let me know at richardbaconmp@parliament.uk.

SELF-BUILD LAUNCH
Yesterday I chaired the launch of a new report by the University of York and Lloyds Bank on the future of Self-Build housing. Self-Build is a small sector in the UK - in fact, much smaller than most of our continental neighbours. Examples from self-build projects in Germany and the Netherlands show it is not just wealthy people who can benefit.  Whole communities can use self-build as a way of addressing the shortage in affordable houses without resorting to volume-built estates with no sense of identity.

We can do much more in this area, helping to solve the housing crisis while protecting our cherished environment. That is why I am also launching a new All-Party Parliamentary Group on Self Build and Independent Housebuilding.

The UK’s self-build market is small, but it has great potential.  It is encouraging that a mortgage provider with Lloyds’ clout is looking at self-build seriously.  

LOCALISM
Diss Town Coucil and its Clerk and her team do a sterling job for Diss.  The Council has a very clear idea of our town’s potential and it works closely with local business partners to make Diss a lovely place to live.

However, I want keen to see capable town councils like Diss get the power to do more for their residents.  Central government needs to get out of the way of local councils.

The localism agenda offers so many opportunities for innovative, energetic town councils like Diss.   I want the Government to help our town to do more and, now Parliament has returned, I will be talking to ministers with that aim in mind. 

17 May 2013
