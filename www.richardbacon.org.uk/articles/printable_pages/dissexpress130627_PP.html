BACON BITS: Richard's monthly Diss Express column


South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community.

HEROES HONOURED AT LAST
Last week the Government announced that veterans who served on the Arctic Convoys in World War II will be allowed to accept the Ushakov Medal from the Russian Government.

The Medal, named after a Russian naval hero who fought with Nelson,  is given to all Arctic Convoy veterans.  US and Commonwealth veterans can already wear the Medal but bizarre Foreign Office rules said that, as the event to be honoured was over five years ago and Britain had already awarded them the Arctic Star, British veterans couldn’t accept the Medal.    

I was pleased to play some small part in the campaign to allow veterans to accept the Russian peoples’ recognition of their service so I’m delighted that the Government has now done the right thing by these extraordinarily brave men. 

LONG STRATTON
Getting a bypass for Long Stratton is one of my top priorities as your local MP and if you live, work or travel through Long Stratton every day, then you’ll know that the village desperately needs to be bypassed. 

Long Stratton will need to grow if the bypass is to be delivered and this growth will also provide new jobs, better services and improved facilities.  In order to guide this growth, South Norfolk Council has drawn up a detailed draft Area Action Plan for Long Stratton. 

The Council is currently taking comments on the Long Stratton Area Action Plan at www.south-norfolk.gov.uk/longstratton.  The consultation will end at 5pm on Friday 26 July and I’ll be putting my comments in to the Council very soon.  I hope that you’ll do the same. 

TELL THE TAXMAN
Small business owners in my constituency often tell me they’re fed up with HM Revenue & Customs crushing them with an iron fist whilst huge multinational firms like Vodafone get the gentle touch. 

HMRC recently asked MPs  for our views on its performance but, as far as I am concerned, it’s your views that matter most. 

I want to give HMRC chapter and verse on its service to my constituents and so I’m going to need your help.  If you’re a small business owner or an ordinary taxpayer in my constituency and you want to share your experiences of HMRC , be they good or bad, then I want to hear from you. 

Please email me at tellthetaxman@richardbacon.org.uk and I will make sure HMRC hears what you have to say. 

27 June 2013
