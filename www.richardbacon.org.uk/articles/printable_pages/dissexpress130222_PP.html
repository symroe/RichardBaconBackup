BACON BITS: Richard's monthly Diss Express column

HORSEMEAT SCANDAL

Many of you will have been shocked to learn that 100 percent of the meat content in some ready meals has been found to come from horses. 

So many contractors and sub-contractors are involved, it’s very difficult to trace where the horsemeat first entered the food chain.  Small wonder that shoppers are worried.

Regular readers will know that I have campaigned for better food labelling for many years.  Now the scandal of horsemeat in processed meat products has reignited this debate. 

It’s completely unacceptable that mislabelled meat has entered the UK food chain.  Processed meat products must have clearer labelling so that consumers know exactly what’s in them, including the species from which the meat came.     

If you’re in any doubt, look for the red tractor mark and Buy British! 

RAIL

You don’t need me to tell you that Norfolk has struggled to get its infrastructure updated for the 21st Century.  The county’s MPs have been fighting hard for much-needed improvements.

After successes with the A11 dualling and Norfolk’s broadband network, attention is turning to the railways.   Network Rail recently published its investment plans for 2014-2019.  Although the plans aspire to make journeys from Diss to London quicker, they don’t commit to achieving this. 

The plans need at least to make a five-minute cut in journey times by 2019 and build extra tracks to ease the bottleneck around Chelmsford and outer London. 

I’ve called on the Transport Secretary and the Rail Regulator to ensure that Network Rail commits to making these changes by 2019.

I’ll let you know what they say.

MATERNITY SERVICES

The National Audit Office are reviewing NHS maternity services and they’ve asked me to help them find out what new mums think. 

If you’ve recently used the NHS’s maternity services, or if you work in the NHS, then I want to hear from you.  This is your chance to share your experiences and shape an important national review of how maternity services are performing.   MPs will use this report to hold NHS bosses to account. 

If you would like to share your experiences, good or bad, then please do get in touch.  You can email me at inquiries@richardbacon.org.uk, putting ‘maternity services’ in the subject line and including your full contact details and the name of the hospital you are writing about. 

All submissions will be treated in the strictest confidence.

22 February 2013
