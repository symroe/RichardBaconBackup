Concerns over Chinese lanterns persist

South Norfolk MP Richard Bacon brings Diss Express readers up to date with some of his recent work in the community.

Fears are being raised again that Chinese lanterns are a serious fire risk to property and arable crops as well as being dangerous to farm animals. New calls are being made for Chinese lanterns to be banned.

Early last year I wrote to the then Minister at the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills to raise the concerns of many farmers in South Norfolk as to the hazards posed by these lanterns.  Then last September I raised the matter at Prime Minister’s Questions where I was assured that the government was contacting lantern manufacturers to demand that the lanterns should carry full safety instructions and be 100 per cent biodegradable.

However, I am still hearing about fires taking place, putting crops and property at risk. I have now written to ministers to raise these concerns and to ask what action the government has taken so far and what further steps are planned.

Long Stratton Police Station
Many people travelling through Long Stratton on the A140 will have seen the village’s impressive new police station, which I was recently invited to visit by Sergeant Tim Chapman.  It is a striking building with modern office space, an interview room and a crew room for local paramedics who use the station as a base.  

The locker room was full of crime-fighting kit, even including riot gear, and I was surprised to learn that Sergeant Chapman goes up to Coltishall periodically to practise having petrol bombs thrown at him!

High Speed Tour
Group Lotus is one of the largest employers in South Norfolk.  Over the last year I have repeatedly urged Business Secretary Vince Cable to visit Lotus personally to see for himself the company’s extraordinary work, both as a car manufacturer and as an engineering adviser to many of the world’s major motor corporations. 

So I was extremely pleased that earlier this week Vince finally paid a visit to the Lotus headquarters, to hear about the company’s exciting plans, including the new range of cars planned for the next four years.   Lotus are leading the way with hybrid cars using the latest fuel cell and battery technology, which will have much wider use than just for sports cars.  I hope that company’s relatively small voluntary redundancy programme will prove a blip on an exciting upward path for Lotus.  Certainly, Vince showed no signs of slowing down when he drove a Lotus Evora on the new test track!

22 July 2011
